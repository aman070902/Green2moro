Burying CO2 and Saving the Planet.

Carbon sequestration is the process of capturing and storing carbon dioxide (CO2) from the atmosphere in order to reduce the amount of greenhouse gases and mitigate the effects of climate change.

Biological sequestration:

This method involves the storage of carbon in plants and soils through activities such as reforestation, afforestation, and improved land management practices.

Explore Now!

Geological sequestration:

This method involves the injection of CO2 into geological formations such as deep saline aquifers or depleted oil and gas reservoirs.

Explore Now!

Ocean sequestration:

This method involves the injection of CO2 into the ocean, where it can be stored in the form of dissolved CO2 or converted into bicarbonate ions.

Explore Now!

Industrial sequestration:

This method involves capturing CO2 emissions from industrial processes such as power generation, cement production, and steel manufacturing and storing them underground.
