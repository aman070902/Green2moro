The Carbon Market

How Businesses are investing in a Greener Future?

Carbon credits are a form of tradeable certificate or permit that represents the right to emit one metric ton of carbon dioxide or its equivalent in other greenhouse gases.

Carbon Credits:

These credits can be bought and sold on carbon markets, such as the European Union Emissions Trading System (EU ETS) and the Chicago Climate Exchange (CCX), with the goal of reducing overall greenhouse gas emissions by allowing companies and organizations to offset their emissions by purchasing credits from entities that have emitted less than their allotted amount. The idea is that the market will create an economic incentive for companies and organizations to reduce their emissions, as they can then sell their unused credits to others. The carbon credit market is a part of a larger effort to combat climate change by reducing global greenhouse gas emissions. The use of carbon credits is a way for countries and businesses to meet their emissions reduction targets under the 1997 United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) and the 2015 Paris Agreement.
